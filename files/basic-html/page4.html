 Principal   Mrs Lemmer
 Principal




 2020 has most certainly been a year that has brought about huge
 challenges.  However, 2020 has also provided great opportunities.
 I am immensely proud of every single Amanzimtoti High learner.
 Thank you very much for the way in which you have accepted the
 reality of 2020.
 It was not always easy, but you did it – and the result is that you
 have evolved stronger and far greater at the end of it all.

 This year will form part of History–and you will be a major part of it.
 The hardships that you faced this year certainly contributed so
     much to who you are today.
 Attitude is everything in life.  Your attitude determines your altitude.  I fully believe that the
 attitude that you approached your challenges with this year, is what has made all the diﬀerence.
 You are a dedicated group of learners, displaying tenacity and courage.
 I know you will be able to approach your future head on and achieve huge successes.

 Remember to never stop moving forward.  On this path of life, many of us will stumble along
  the way.  Get up, dust yourself oﬀ and continue, no matter how dark it may seem at the time.
 There is always a light ahead.  Just dig deep into your inner strength and let your own light
 shine even brighter.
 The greatest thing that you can do for yourself is to use every opportunity.
 Continue to be the hero that you are destined to be.
 Always strive to be the better version of yourself.
 Bruce Lee said:  ”Pain is a privilege.  Use it to become stronger, bolder and better.”
 Always remember the words of Jeremiah 29:11:  “For I know the plans I have for you, declares
 the Lord, plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you hope and a future”.
 With my best wishes,
 Deputy Principal  Mr Kapp
 Deputy Principal
 Mrs Lemmer


 Saying goodbye to our Matrics is always a bittersweet moment.
 On the one hand, we are happy that you have achieved your goal
 and can move on to better things, on the other, it is sad to part
 ways. During the past ﬁve years, we have got to know most of you
 quite well. We met your parents, guardians, other family members.
 We learned many family stories, some personality traits, your
 strengths and your weaknesses. For ﬁve years, we tried, to the best
 of our ability, to give you the most suitable tools for your future.
 We may have made mistakes along the way, but what I can
 promise you is that we had your best interests at heart. I hope
 that all of you felt in your time here, that Amanzimtoti High School
 was not only a place where you were taught subject knowledge, but
 also a place where you learned values and a place where you made friends for life.
 This year has tested all of us in so many ways and yet one thought continues to ring true more
 than ever, “Nihil sine Labore”, our school’s motto. It took you twelve years of hard work and
 dedication to get to where you are today and we celebrate this great achievement with you.
 Nonetheless, this is the beginning of a new chapter in your life, and you must remember
 that whether you continue studying or you start working, nothing worth achieving will
 come without labour.
 Matrics of 2020, I wish you all the best for the future professionally as well as personally.
 May you ﬁnd your passion in life and work hard to pursue it to the fullest!
